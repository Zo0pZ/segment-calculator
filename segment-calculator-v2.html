Max Segments Encashable
Without top slicing — the full chargeable gain from each encashed segment is assessed against income in the year of encashment. The maximum number of segments is therefore limited by the target gain divided by the gain per segment.

With top slicing — the gain is divided by the number of complete years the bond has been held (the term) to calculate the effective income for rate-banding purposes. This typically allows a greater number of segments to be encashed while remaining within the target.
